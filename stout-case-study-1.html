Case Study 1¶

💰 Lending Club Analysis of Loans¶

(10000, 55)

Lending CLub allows individuals to lend money to other individuals. This data set includes the loans that were processed and not the loan applications. Hence, any loans that were not offered or not offer not accepted are not included.

A data frame with 10,000 observations on the following 55 variables.

Visualizing Data¶

Top Professions lending on the platform¶

Text(0.5, 1.0, 'Top 10 Professions Loaning on the Platform ')

From the above plot it is evident what the applicants on the platform do for living. A huge chunk of loans are taken by managers and business owners but surprisingly even teachers make a huge chunk of the data set. As of writing this notebook, the job title "rn" is currently unknown but it is a part of the top 10.

Grade and Interest Rate¶

ASSUMPTION: The grades are assigned as per interest rate and they are used to divide the interest rate into classes

Text(0.5, 1.0, 'Interest rates distributes as per grades')

As we can see our assumptions of grade and interest rate relationship hold. From the above graph we can see the number of applicants drop after 15% interest rate. And then there is huge spike with Grade G applicants. These are very risky loan applicants having a very high interest rate. As the applicants become risker they drop from taking the loan and its seen with the numbers.

Loan Amounts in Grades¶

It is important to understand if the loan amount has effect on the grade and in turn the loan amount. On plotting the loan amount distribution for each grade it is very evident the peak of the distribution shifts towards the right as the grade increases. Loans with higher interest rate are often against a higher loan amount. The shift in the peak can be seen gradually happen from grade A-E.

Top Purpose for getting a loan¶

Text(0.5, 1.0, 'Top 10 Reasons For Getting A Loan')

It can be seen that people on the platform about half of them are getting loans for the purpose of paying out other loans. The half of the remaining are using the loans to pay off their credit cards. This makes sense as Lending Club is a platform unlike a financial institution which would term debt consolidation a risky purpose to lend money.
